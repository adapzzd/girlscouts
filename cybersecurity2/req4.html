Find Out How Information Online can Last Forever

What types of things last forever online?

Everything. Everything online lasts forever. Even old versions of websites last forever. I used the Wayback Machine to view an old version of the Girlscouts website and compared it to the more recent version. You might be farmiliar with Snapchat. Snapchat's privacy policy says that after 30 days, your messages are completely deleted, even from Snapchat's server. But someone could easity save that message by taking a screen photo of your chat. Then, they could post it online or save it to their photos, and then it really will last forever. So, everything lasts forever online. That is why you should always think before you post, even if it will be 'deleted' by Snapchat or another company.

Can you look through the information that a company has about you?

In some places. In Europe, you can call up a company and look through all of the information a company has about you, and you can even ask them to delete it. That is called 'The Right to be Forgotten'. In the United States, we don't have that right. That means that Facebook or Twitter or Instagram can gather information about us and we won't even know what they have. If you have a social media account and you want all of your information deleted, the best thing to do is create a new account. That's the closest you can get. Facebook would probably be able to trace you back to your new account, based on the things you post, though.

If I don't have a Facebook account, does Facebook know anything about me?

Yes, they probably know some things about you. Even if you don't have an account, Facebook probably has a seceret 'shadow account' for you. They get this information about you from people you know. No, your friends and family are not selling your information to Facebook. When you create an account, Facebook asks you is you want to allow access to your contacts. Most people say yes. When Facebook goes into your contacts, it looks for someone who does not have a facebook account. That's you! They pull off your name, email, and other information, maybe even where you live, and put that into your 'shadow account'. If your friend posts something about you, say, how jealous she was of your vacation to Hawaii, Facebook will put that in your 'shadow account' too. And the scary thing is, you won't even know it's there. How does this fit in the topic of information lasting forever? Your 'shadow account' will last forever and Facebook will probably keep it forever.

What is computational thinking?

Wikipedia: Computational Thinking

Computational thinking is the process of breaking up a problem you don't know how to solve or a process you don't know how to complete into bits that you do know how to complete.

How can I think computationally?

All you have to do is turn a complex task into a list of short, easy tasks. For example, how to make a sandwich:

To make a sandwich:
  1. Find and open a bag of bread.
  2. Take out two slices of bread and lay them side by side.
  3. Find a butterknife. Place it next to the bread.
  4. Find a jar of peanut butter and open it.
  5. Find a jar of jelly and open it.
  6. Scoop jelly from the jelly jar and spread it on a bread slice.
  7. Scoop peanut butter from the peanut butter jar and spread it on a bread slice.
  8. Bring the two slices of bread together so that the peanut butter and jelly are facing inwards.

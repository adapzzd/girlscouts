4. Explore nature at night

DATE

I looked for Comet Leonard in the very early morning. I couldn't find it, but I did find Polaris and where the comet was supposed to be. It was super hard to even find where it was supposed to be! When I looked there I couldn't see anything. In the USA today article I read about it, it says anyone in the world can see it with some binoculars or a telescope. One of the problems might have been all of the utility wires blocking the sky. It was also close to sunrise, so it may have been really dim. Even though I didn't get to see the comet, it was still really fun to get up early and go stargazing! After looking for the comet, I watched a video on CNN that says the difference between comets, meteors, meteorites, and asteroids. Here's what I learned:

  • Comets are pieces of frozen gas with dust and rocks in them.
  • Asteroids are rocks, with hard surfaces. They come from the asteroid belt.
  • Meteors are asteroids that hit the earth's atmosphere and burn up.
  • Meteorites are asteroids that hit the earth. The ones that don't are called shooting stars.
